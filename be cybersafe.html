Learn How To Be Cyber-Safe!

Prevent Cyberbullying

  • Use in-app privacy tools and settings
    In todays world, almost all social media apps have the option to make your account private, or not
    allow strangers to send you direct messages. You can make use of this and stop cyberbullying
    before it can even happen to you.
  • Keep personal information private
    Before posting, or sharing something, make sure you don’t share your address, the places you
    visit normally such as school, or a cafe you visit pretty often, or even your last name. addresses
    can make it pretty easy for cyberbullies to target you and start stalking you in real life.
  • Think before posting/commenting
    Posting impulsive posts is an open door for cyberbullies to walk in from. Even if you post something
    and delete it immediately, there’s still the risk that someone took a screenshot from it, and shared it
    with others. It’s always best to double check and think about what you are going to post or comment.
  • Try to limit your posts and screen-time on social media
    You can easily attract the attention of cyberbullies if you're one of those people who post a lot of
    pictures and selfies. The pictures and everyday routines that you post gives them a lot of material to
    start their work with.
  • Log out from your account in public devices
    if you are using a public computer, and you’re logged into your social media account through the
    public device, make sure to log out of your account before leaving. Delete your password from the
    web browser after you are done with it. Simply closing the site is not enough. Cyberbullies can easily
    walk up to the device you were using, see your account, change your password and start impersonating
    you.

Already a Victim?

  • Report
    Most social platforms have the option to report an offensive/harmful post or message. If you are getting
    cyberbullied publicly, this is one of the things you must do. Report the post that is about you. Tell your
    friends to also report the post. This way they will remove the post from their platforms and give a warning
    to the bully.
  • Block
    If you are getting cyberbullied via private chat, then the best option is to quickly report and block them. By
    doing this, they will receive a warning/get their account suspended, and no longer will be able to send you
    messages.
  • Do Not Respond
    Simply ignore them. Do not respond to anything they say. You can even log out for some time. Cyberbullies
    mostly want to see the reaction of their victim. They want to drive a response out of their targets. When you
    show no reaction and ignore them, they get bored and stop targeting you
  • Save Evidence
    Save all the conversations between yourself and the cyberbully. Screenshot any harmful or offensive posts
    and whatever they tell you in private chat. This will come in handy whenever you asked for help from a trusted
    adult.
  • Tell a Trusted Adult
    You should not hesitate to talk about this with a trusted adult or your close friends. Friends can support you
    emotionally and trusted adults such as parents or teachers can give you advice on how to face the situation.
    Show any evidence you have from the cyberbullying posts or messages to them.
