What is Cyberbullying?

Cyberbullying, is basically any type of bullying that can happen online through
social media platforms, such as TikTok, Instagram, Snapchat, Discord, etc. where
users can share, post and comment. Cyberbullies will use these features to share
negative, harmful, and even humiliating and false information posts about their
victim, with the goal to hurt, threaten, or embarrass them. You might think that its
not such big of a deal, but it can have a great impact on the victim’s life.

Types of Cyberbullying

Roasting

This kind of cyberbullying
involves the cyberbully posting or sending direct messages, which contains insults and profanity, to their target. Be aware that many cyberbullies don’t realize
what they are doing
is wrong.

Masquerading

The bully will create a fake identity, and start harassing their target through texting. The bully can stay anonymous, and use someone else’s identity.

Trickery

The bully here will try to get
close to the victim slowly and extract personal information about the victim. Then they will take advantage of this and leak the private information online.

Trolling

When trolling, the bully intentionally posts offensive posts or comments online to harass the victim. The bully may not have any personal relationships with the victim.

Cyberstalking

Cyberstalking is a serious form
of cyberbullying. The bully can monitor the target, make false accusations, and it can even lead to offline stalking. It might extend to physical threats to the targeted victim.

Doxing/Outing

Similar to trickery, the bullies here try to collect the victim’s personal information like their home address, contact information or social security number. They don’t ask the victim for information directly, but they threaten the victim by leaking their personal information.

What Makes Teens Want To Cyberbully Others?

Usually, a bully’s motivation stems from their own feelings, problems and/or situation. generally, teens who bully others are
from 2 different groups: the ones who are popular and those who are less socially active or successful. Those who are popular
may bully others for more recognition and in some cases, hurting others makes them feel more powerful. On the other hand, those
who are less successful socially may bully others to help them cope with their own problems and low self-esteem, or they think that
they can fit in with their peers better if they bullied others. Another factor is that teens and kids who bully others have less
involved parents for whatever reason, are less excited about school, and/or are anxious or depressed. Bullies take advantage of the
anonymity of social media and the fact that they can’t get caught, and hurt others however they want. Some even think that
cyberbullying is funny, since they don’t see the victim’s reaction in person.
